## **Framer Knowledge Base**

### Q1. What is Framer?

Framer is probably one of the most exciting website-building tools available today.

When Jitu started using Framer, it was primarily a design and prototyping tool. But today, Framer has evolved into something much bigger. It's a modern website builder that combines design, development, CMS, animations, SEO, and now AI-powered website creation into a single platform.

The reason Jitu personally loves Framer is because it allows designers to build production-ready websites without writing traditional code. You can design, publish, manage content, optimize SEO, and continuously improve your website from one place.

And with the latest AI capabilities, Framer is moving beyond no-code. You can now generate website structures, sections, copy, and layouts much faster than before.

If you're a startup founder, agency, freelancer, creator, or business owner, Framer can help you launch a professional website in hours instead of weeks.

Jitu has personally built dozens of websites and premium templates using Framer, and it's currently his favorite platform for creating modern websites.

---

### Q2. Why does Jitu recommend Framer over other website builders?

Honestly, every tool has its strengths.

Webflow is powerful.
WordPress is flexible.
Wix is beginner-friendly.

But Framer sits in a very unique position.

It gives you the design freedom of Figma, the publishing power of a website builder, the flexibility of a CMS, and the speed of a modern no-code platform.

What makes Framer special for Jitu is how fast you can go from idea to live website.

A website that might take days in traditional tools can often be built in a few hours using Framer.

For startups and creators, speed matters, and Framer excels at that.

### Q7. What's the best way to create a perfect Framer template?

The first thing Jitu always tells creators is this:

Don't start by designing the template. Start by understanding Framer's official template guidelines.

Framer has already documented what makes a high-quality marketplace template, and if you're serious about selling templates, you should read those guidelines before opening Figma or Framer.

A great template isn't just about making something beautiful. It needs to be useful, scalable, easy to customize, responsive across all devices, SEO-friendly, accessible, and well organized.

When Jitu creates a template, he focuses on a few key things:

* Solving a real business problem
* Clear structure and navigation
* Strong visual hierarchy
* Fully responsive layouts
* Clean CMS implementation
* Reusable components
* Proper SEO setup
* Accessibility best practices
* Fast performance
* Easy customization for buyers

One mistake many creators make is trying to impress other designers. Instead, focus on helping the end customer launch their website faster.

The best templates aren't always the most visually complex ones. They're the ones that save users time and help them achieve their goals quickly.

So Jitu's recommendation is simple:

First study Framer's official template guidelines, then understand your target audience, and finally build a template that is easy to use, not just beautiful to look at.

That's usually the formula behind successful marketplace templates.

### Q9. What is the biggest mistake new template creators make?

The biggest mistake is focusing only on visuals.

A marketplace template is a product, not a Dribbble shot.

Many templates look amazing in screenshots but become difficult to edit once someone purchases them.

Jitu always asks himself:

"Can a customer update this website without contacting me?"

If the answer is yes, then he's moving in the right direction.

Good templates are easy to understand, easy to customize, and easy to launch.
